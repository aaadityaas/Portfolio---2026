OVERVIEW

Zapp Account is a complete rebrand and ground-up rebuild of PayZapp Wallet. The product was rewritten in Flutter, redesigned from first principles, and given a fresh visual identity. This case study covers the journey from a struggling legacy wallet to a fintech product that grew adoption through thoughtful design and strategic GTM execution.

PayZapp Wallet existed but had poor adoption rates. Users weren't engaging with the core features. When the decision came to rebrand and rebuild, we treated it as a blank slate — new name, new design system, new code. The product needed to be faster, clearer, and genuinely delightful.

01 — The Problem

Existed, worked, nobody used it.

PayZapp Wallet was the predecessor. It existed, it worked, but nobody used it. The UI felt dated, the flows were confusing, and the overall experience didn't feel modern or trustworthy. Users had to navigate through too many screens to do simple things. The design didn't communicate the value of the product. It was time to start from zero.

[ PayZapp Wallet — original product screens ]

What we needed to fix

  • Visual language felt corporate and cold
  • Onboarding was multi-step and friction-heavy
  • Core actions were buried in sub-menus
  • Trust indicators were missing from key screens
  • Mobile experience felt like a port from web

02 — The Rebranding Decision

Don't patch it. Rebuild it.

When leadership decided to rebrand and relaunch, we made a critical choice: don't patch the old product. Rebuild it entirely. New name (Zapp Account), new design system, new codebase in Flutter. This wasn't just a UI refresh — it was a complete reimagining of what the product should be.

Flutter gave us the ability to move fast across iOS and Android without duplicating effort. The design needed to be modern, trustworthy, and fast.

[ Visual identity, color palette, type, and component library ]

03 — Design Iterations & Problem Solving

Solving one problem at a time.

We went through multiple rounds of iteration. Each one solved a specific problem. The home screen needed to show wallet balance and quick actions at a glance. Payments needed a clear, single-step flow. Onboarding had to be fast but still gather the necessary KYC info. We explored different approaches, tested them, and iterated.

Home & Dashboard

The heart of the product. We needed to balance information density with clarity. Where does the balance live? How prominent should quick actions be? We went through 4–5 major iterations before landing on the right hierarchy.

Onboarding & KYC

New users needed to get into the app fast, but KYC verification was a hard requirement. We introduced Video KYC as a way to make the compliance step feel seamless and quick. No wait times, instant verification.

04 — The Design Journey

Planner, executor, bridge.

My role evolved throughout the project. Early on, I was heads-down on design planning — defining the problem space, building the design system, and creating initial concepts. But as the project progressed and we moved into execution, I shifted gears. I became the bridge between design and engineering, sitting with developers several times a week to solve pixel-perfect implementation challenges.

Every interaction, every spacing detail, every animation had to feel right. The final weeks were about quality assurance — reviewing builds, catching inconsistencies, and making sure the shipped product matched the design. It was intense, rewarding, and taught me how to move fast without sacrificing craft.

05 — Zapp Account

This is what shipped.

A modern, trustworthy, fast wallet experience. The visual design is clean and purposeful. The interactions are smooth. The flows are frictionless. But most importantly, people are using it. Adoption grew, transaction volume increased, and user retention is strong. The rebrand worked — not just visually, but behaviorally.

06 — What Could Come Next

Designed but not shipped.

During design, we explored features and ideas that never made it to launch. Savings goals, investment features, credit products, spending insights — all of these were explored and designed, but didn't ship in the first version. They're possibilities we'd love to pursue if the product roadmap allows. This section shows the thinking behind what could be next for Zapp Account.

Savings · investments · spending insights — designed, not shipped.

Why these didn't ship

Prioritization, technical constraints, and MVP scope. Not abandoned — deferred for future iterations.

Reflections

This project taught me about moving fast without sacrificing quality. Working across design, product, and engineering required constant communication and collaboration. The biggest win wasn't the rebrand itself — it was proving that thoughtful design, when paired with a clear product vision and strong engineering execution, can actually move the needle on user behavior.

Zapp Account isn't just prettier than PayZapp Wallet. It's faster to use, easier to understand, and genuinely more trustworthy. That's what happens when you're willing to start from zero.
